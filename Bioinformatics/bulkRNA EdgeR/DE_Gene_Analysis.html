  • Fraction of rRNA < 10%
  • Percantage of Uniquely Mapped Reads > 85%

logcpm_before <- cpm(y, log=TRUE)

The key point is to finding a constant for each of the samples that by multiplying all the count values in the samples for constant the trend is to have most of the genes not to change expression;

After Normalization: Median of all replicates become in more less equal value. As you can see, outliers didn’t change. the change is happens in the middle of boxplot. What you have in the middle? the genes that do not change their expression. The shift is a linear shift because each of the counts are multiplied by constant (norm.factors). This assumption is based on most of the genes will not change their expression in a significant way.

Differential Expression Analysis:

Estimate Dispersion: This plot explains the relationship between the average expression and the variability. Underline statistical model is NB distribution. The variance of the NB, depends on a key parameter which is called dispersion. Biological coefficient of variation is basically the square root of the dispersion. So the bigger is this coefficient, the higher variability and variance of the corresponding gene.

y <- estimateDisp(y, design) 

plotBCV(y, main=("Relationship between mean/variance of gene expression"))

Apply a Test to make comparison between genes in according to their expression profile;

fit <- glmQLFit(y, design) 

#kidney (top) vs brain (bottom)
qlfKB <- glmQLFTest(fit, contrast=c(-1,1,0)) #in this particular test, I test whether there is differential expression between top(1,kidney) bottom(-1,brain). so if they change is positive meaning more in kidney than in brain, if the change is negative it means more in brain than kidney.
